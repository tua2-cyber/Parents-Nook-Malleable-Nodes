# Diagram: The Nook + App Relationship

This diagram describes the relationship between the real-world Nook and the technology layer.

The app should not control the Nook.

The app should help remember what humans notice.

```text
THE NOOK
local • human • relational

children play
parents stay nearby
facilitators observe
trust forms slowly
        ↓
HUMAN OBSERVATION
parents notice
facilitators adjust
spaces shape behavior
children respond
        ↓
APP AS MEMORY LAYER
stores signals + context
supports decisions
does not replace judgment
        ↓
NOOK NETWORK
local learning stays local
useful patterns travel
prescriptions do not
trust is not centralized
        ↓
THE NOOK
adapts in context
```

## Notes

The Nook remains the center.

Technology supports the Nook. It does not replace the relationships inside it.

The system should help parents, facilitators, and spaces notice patterns while keeping trust local.

The goal is not automation of care.

The goal is better memory, better coordination, and better learning across local care nodes.

## What the app remembers

The app can help remember things like:

- family interest and readiness
- parent questions
- age range and session fit
- attendance and repeat participation
- facilitator observations
- space constraints
- what helped a child settle
- what made a session feel calm or stretched
- pricing and sustainability signals

## What the app should not replace

The app should not replace:

- human judgment
- facilitator intuition
- parent trust
- local rhythm
- the feeling of safety inside a room
- the relationships that form through repetition

## Design principle

The app is not the Nook.

The app is a memory layer around the Nook.

The Nook stays local, human, and adaptive.
